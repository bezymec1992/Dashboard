Understanding the Solar System

The solar system consists of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, including eight planets, their moons, and smaller objects such as dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets. The planets are divided into two groups: the inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars) and the outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune).

The Inner Planets

The inner planets are also known as terrestrial planets because they have solid, rocky surfaces. They are much smaller than the outer planets and have shorter orbits around the Sun.

The Outer Planets

The outer planets, also known as gas giants, are much larger than the inner planets and have thick atmospheres composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. They also have numerous moons and rings.
